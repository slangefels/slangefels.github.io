Legacy & Community Impact

Manufacturing and sale of the Nintendo 3DS was discontinued on September 16, 2020. The 3DS eShop, the online storefront where video game software for the 3DS systems could be bought and downloaded, was closed on March 27, 2023. As reported by Nintendo, the Nintendo 3DS family sold 75.94 million units of hardware and 392.28 million units of software as of June 30, 2025.

Despite Nintendo discontinuing the 3DS and its eShop in favor of focusing on marketing its next big console, the Nintendo Switch, the 3DS console family is still looked upon with affection by fans today. Searching "Nintendo 3DS" on YouTube will result in plenty of videos with titles such as "Why the Nintendo 3DS Is Still Amazing" (Garrett Buffington); "I bought a new 3DS instead of the Switch 2" (Yanterko); "Why you SHOULD Buy a Nintendo 3DS in 2025" (Mani's Tech Shack); "The 3DS is Special" (Justin's Game Corner); "a modded 3DS is nearly perfect" (bigsleeter); and more. The existence of these videos exemplifies how the handheld gaming community is still interested in the 3DS even if Nintendo has moved on from it.


In recent years, since the Switch became Nintendo's primarly console and the 3DS was discontinued, the 3DS has seemed to have a renaissance in online communities in part due to how easy it is to mod the older console.

Why has the 3DS seemingly had a resurgence in popularity since its discontinuing, especially when Nintendo has released powehouse consoles in its wake such as the Switch and Switch 2?

The two largest factors for the renewed interest in the 3DS are likely nostalgia and the 3DS's ability to be easily modified.

Nostalgia

The 3DS was first released in 2011, making the console 14 years old at this point in time. By now, the people who grew up playing on the 3DS are around 20-30 years old and have reach a period in their lives where the objects and toys of their childhood have become nostalgic relics.

According to the University of Florida Health, "As we transition into adulthood, nostalgia evolves into a useful coping mechanism for managing lifeâ€™s pressures, such as career changes, starting a family or navigating other significant milestones." As these young adults assimilate into the work force and become financially independent they may be inclined to invest in buying back toys from their youth, such as a Nintendo 3DS, for the sake of comfort and to reminisce on simpler times.

Modding

Modding (short for "modifying") is described by Wikipedia as "...the act of modifying hardware, software, or anything else to perform a function not originally intended by the designer, or to achieve bespoke specification or appearance. The term is often used in reference to video game modding, particularly in regard to creating new or altered content and sharing that via the web."

3DS has a healthy modding community thanks to the device's relative ease and versatility when it comes to being modded. "With custom firmware, you can import custom themes for the menus, export saves for transfer to a different system, or even back up your games directly on the SD card so that you can play them without the need for a physical cartridge" writes Zachary Cimaglio for HowToGeek.com. Modding a 3DS can also enable it to use emulators that allow players to play games originally platformed on retro systems like the Super Nintendo, Game Boy Advance, etc.

Modding breaths new life into the console as it lets players customize their systems and play games that may no longer be accessible through traditional means due to the closure of the eShop or may be too expensive to buy physical copies of.
